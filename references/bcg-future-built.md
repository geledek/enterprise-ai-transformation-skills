# BCG Build for the Future × AI 2026

## What this reference contains

BCG's Build for the Future × AI 2026 flagship research. Core findings on the AI value distribution (70/20/10), the manager role-modeling gap (88/25), and the leadership behaviors that distinguish AI high performers from the majority.

## Key claims (with citations)

- **70/20/10 value distribution.** 70% of AI value comes from People, 20% from Process, 10% from Technology. Source: BCG BFFxAI 2026.
- **AI high performers are 3× more likely to be "Future-Built"** — embedding AI as a core organizational capability, not just a tool. Source: BCG BFFxAI 2026.
- **88% of managers believe role-modeling matters; only 25% do it visibly.** The 63-point gap is the largest single behavioral lever in the People layer. Source: BCG BFFxAI 2026.
- **Leaders who invest in ALL three dimensions (strategy, processes, people) simultaneously outperform** those who invest in any single dimension. Source: BCG BFFxAI 2026.
- **Change management is the most consistently under-resourced dimension.** AI investments cluster in technology; change management and people investments are systematically under-weighted. Source: BCG BFFxAI 2026.
- **Future-Built firms are 2× more likely to achieve significant AI-driven revenue growth** vs. Traditionalists. Source: BCG BFFxAI 2026.

## The Future-Built vs. Traditionalist distinction

**Future-Built firms:**
- AI embedded in business strategy, not siloed in IT
- CEO personally owns AI outcomes
- Mandatory AI training across the org
- Test-and-learn is the operating default
- Change management is a first-class investment

**Traditionalist firms:**
- AI as a series of IT or data-team experiments
- No CEO-level ownership
- Training is optional and awareness-only
- Pilots succeed but rarely scale
- Change management is an afterthought

## When to consult

- For skill `readiness-conversation`: the Future-Built/Traditionalist diagnostic is the rapid senior-team positioning tool.
- For skill `maturity-assessment`: BCG's Future-Built archetype maps against Accenture's AI Achiever quadrant and MIT CISR's Stage 3–4.
