# PwC Six-Step AI ROI Roadmap

## What this reference contains

PwC's sequential six-step pathway from AI aspiration to measurable ROI. The roadmap integrates strategic alignment, governance, pilot discipline, and reinvention into a single arc. It is both a planning tool and a maturity diagnostic.

## The six steps

| Step | Focus | Key action |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Align to ONE business goal** | Quantify: efficiency, growth, or innovation. Don't pursue all three simultaneously at equal weight. |
| 2 | **Pick a strategic position** | Explicit choice of where to compete in your value chain. Set stopping rules for non-primary positions. |
| 3 | **Build foundation capabilities** | Data platform, governance structure, AI literacy baseline. Without this, pilots cannot scale. |
| 4 | **Launch tracked pilots** | Narrow scope, simple execution. Pre-deployment metrics defined. 90-day target. |
| 5 | **Scale with governance** | Move pilots to production with documented oversight, change management, and measurement. |
| 6 | **Reinvent with AI** | Use AI to change what you sell, not just how you operate. The 2.6× productivity gap lives here. |

Source: PwC Agentic AI Playbook 2026 and PwC ROI 2026.

## Key insights

- **Steps 1–2 are the most skipped.** Most firms move directly to Step 3 (foundation) or Step 4 (pilots) without aligning to a business goal or naming a position. This produces undifferentiated capability that cannot scale.
- **Step 6 requires CEO ownership.** Reinvention is not a technology decision; it is a business-model decision. Without CEO commitment, the org will default to efficiency-only (Step 5 plateau).
- **Roadmap doubles as a maturity diagnostic.** Ask: "Which step are we actually at?" Most firms overestimate by 1–2 steps.

## When to consult

- For skill `pilot-design`: Step 4 defines pilot discipline.
- For skill `roi-gate-pwc`: Steps 1–3 are the pre-gate prerequisites; the 20-item checklist is the Step 4 gate.
- For skill `maturity-assessment`: the six steps map directly against MIT-CISR's four stages and Accenture's archetypes.
