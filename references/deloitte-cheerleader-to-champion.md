# Deloitte — CEO Champion vs. Cheerleader

## What this reference contains

Deloitte 2026 findings on CEO and executive ownership of AI: the difference between a cheerleader (endorses AI publicly but doesn't personally own initiatives) and a champion (personally leads, resources, and is accountable for AI outcomes). Plus: HR's five jobs in the AI workforce transition.

## Key claims (with citations)

- **74% of companies plan to deploy AI agents in the next two years.** Source: Deloitte 2026.
- **Only 21% have mature governance for agentic AI.** The governance-readiness gap is 53 percentage points. Source: Deloitte 2026.
- **CEO champion vs. cheerleader gap is the single largest leadership variable.** McKinsey: firms where the CEO personally owns AI initiatives show a 3× performance differential vs. firms where AI is delegated. Source: McKinsey + Deloitte synthesis.
- **Insufficient worker skills is the #1 barrier to AI integration.** Source: Deloitte 2026.
- **HR's 5 jobs in the AI workforce transition:**
  1. Talent acquisition — AI literacy as a hiring criterion (only ~40% doing this)
  2. Career-path redesign — entry-level roles automating; rebuild the pipeline (only 33% doing this)
  3. Performance and incentive redesign — only 30% combining work patterns from AI usage
  4. Workforce planning and redeployment — assess skill demand (only 30%)
  5. Employment-mix rebalance — FT/contract/gig (only 19%)
  Source: Deloitte 2026, p.14, Figure 4.
- **Stanford AI Index 2026:** 22–25 year-olds in high-AI-exposure occupations are down 16% in headcount. Career-pathway erosion is empirically real, not hypothetical. Source: Stanford AI Index 2026, p.222.

## When to consult

- For skill `readiness-conversation`: HR's 5 jobs define the HR row of the 4-role gap report — ask which of the 5 HR is actively executing vs. deferring.
- For skill `maturity-assessment`: the 74/21 governance gap is a direct indicator of governance maturity — firms that haven't addressed agentic governance are at Stage 2 regardless of pilot count.

## Source

Deloitte AI Leaders Survey 2026. Executive AI ownership research; HR workforce transition findings; governance maturity data.
