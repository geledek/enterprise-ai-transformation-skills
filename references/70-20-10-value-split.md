# 70/20/10 AI Value Split

## What this reference contains

BCG Build for the Future × AI 2026 finding: of the total value AI creates in an enterprise, roughly 70% comes from People, 20% from Process, and 10% from Technology. The distribution is counterintuitive — the layer everyone optimizes (models, tools, infrastructure) produces the smallest value share.

## Key claims (with citations)

- **70% of AI value comes from People** — skills, behaviors, leadership, culture. Source: BCG BFFxAI 2026, core finding.
- **20% comes from Process** — workflow redesign, decision architecture, measurement discipline. Source: BCG BFFxAI 2026.
- **10% comes from Technology** — model selection, infrastructure, tooling. Source: BCG BFFxAI 2026.
- **The 5% that capture nearly all enterprise AI value** allocate effort accordingly; the 95% focus disproportionately on technology. Source: paired finding, MIT NANDA 2025 (see `95-5-genai-divide.md`).

## When to consult

- For skill `readiness-conversation`: anchor the conversation in the 70/20/10 finding when surfacing why people gaps matter disproportionately.
- For skill `stack-diagnostic`: use the 10% figure to calibrate expectations — a perfect tech stack won't rescue a 70% people gap.
- For skill `maturity-assessment`: the 70/20/10 split is the interpretive frame for why Stage 2 → Stage 3 stalls in organizations with strong tech but weak people/process investment.
