# JPMorgan COIN (Contract Intelligence)

**Year:** Deployed 2017 → sustained success → expanded to broader LLM tooling 2024
**Mode pitched:** Augment
**Outcome:** Success — sustained productivity gain; foundation for later AI investment

## Story

In 2017, JPMorgan deployed COIN ("Contract Intelligence"), a machine learning and NLP system that interprets commercial loan agreements. Bloomberg reported the system did in seconds what previously took lawyers and loan officers approximately 360,000 hours per year — extracting roughly 150 attributes from 12,000 commercial credit agreements annually.

The pitch was friction-first and concrete. The pain was named: standardized contract review consumed expensive legal time on low-variance, repetitive work. The mode was Augment — COIN extracted clauses; lawyers reviewed and approved before the output reached any external party.

COIN became a quiet, sustained success. JPMorgan extended the approach into broader legal and operational tooling and, by August 2024, was rolling out the LLM Suite, an internal generative AI platform reportedly available to tens of thousands of employees. The "360,000 hours" figure became a fixture of AI vendor decks; while it originated from JPMorgan's own framing and has not been independently audited, the underlying productivity is widely accepted as real.

## Diagnostic

**Q1 — Real Friction?** Friction-first. Specific, named workflow with measurable hours. The pain belonged to a named team doing a specific task, and the volume was concrete.

**Q2 — Right Solution Mode?** Augment, by design. Output stayed inside JPMorgan and was reviewed by qualified staff before any external action. The reliability test passes — at no point did unreviewed AI output reach a customer, regulator, or counterparty. Critical condition: reviewer trust calibrated to model accuracy on this narrow task. Met.

**Q3 — Value Accumulates?** Improving solution shading toward moat. JPMorgan's own labeled contract corpus is a proprietary asset that compounds across new contract types. A competitor cannot replicate it on day one. Tracked metrics (hours saved, clause-extraction accuracy) align with what matters; the headline figure is marketing-flavored but underlying productivity is real.

**Verdict:** Pass. Narrow, internal, human-reviewed, on a proprietary corpus.

**One change:** Don't let COIN's success license Replace-mode rollouts in higher-stakes legal work. Audit each new domain on its own reliability terms.

## Why it matters as a reference case

COIN is the canonical Augment-mode success. Use this case when arguing for the structural advantages of internal, human-reviewed AI on proprietary data. The four characteristics — narrow scope, internal use, human review, proprietary corpus — are the four conditions that correlate most strongly with sustained AI success across the reference set.

Use it also as a caution against extrapolation. COIN succeeding does not license a Replace-mode rollout into customer-facing legal advice. The mode is what makes it work.
