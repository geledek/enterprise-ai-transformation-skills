# Moderna + ChatGPT Enterprise

**Year:** Announced April 2024
**Mode pitched:** Augment (productivity multiplier)
**Outcome:** Partial Pass — reported success, vendor-amplified, no independent audit

## Story

In April 2024, Moderna announced the deployment of ChatGPT Enterprise to all roughly 16,000 employees. CEO Stéphane Bancel framed AI as foundational to scaling Moderna from one product (the COVID vaccine) to fifteen products in five years without proportional headcount growth.

OpenAI's case study reported that Moderna employees built more than 750 custom GPTs in two months, with adoption around 40 percent. Specific examples included a "Dose ID" GPT used to support clinical trial dose selection, reportedly saving meaningful analyst time. Moderna's Chief Digital and Technology Officer became a frequent speaker at AI conferences, presenting the rollout as a productivity transformation.

By 2025, no independent audit of productivity outcomes had been published. Moderna's broader 2024–2025 financial performance was challenged by post-COVID revenue declines unrelated to AI. The case is real, ongoing, and reported as a success — but the reporting channel is the vendor.

## Diagnostic

**Q1 — Real Friction?** Mixed. Productivity friction across knowledge workers is real but diffuse. The "750 GPTs in two months" framing tilts tech-first — capability theater rather than a named pain.

**Q2 — Right Solution Mode?** Augment. Reliability test passes for most use cases — drafting and summarizing outputs are reviewed by knowledge workers before consequential action; clinical and regulatory outputs sit inside review processes that long predate AI. Critical condition: high-stakes outputs cannot leave the building unreviewed. Met by policy.

**Q3 — Value Accumulates?** Productivity tool. The custom GPTs sit on OpenAI infrastructure; not a moat. Internal know-how on AI workflows is mildly proprietary. Measurement gap is the principal risk — "number of GPTs built" and "% adoption" are tracked because they are easy to track; cycle time on regulatory submissions and scientist time recovered are not, because they are hard. Klarna warning applies.

**Verdict:** Partial Pass. Mode is correct, reviewer culture is strong, but the reported wins are vendor-amplified.

**One change:** Commit to one independently audited outcome metric — for example, regulatory-submission cycle time — and report it annually.

## Why it matters as a reference case

Moderna is the canonical "right mode, weak measurement" case. Use this when a pitch reports adoption metrics (seats deployed, GPTs built, % of staff using) but cannot name an outcome metric tied to business value. Adoption is not value. The Augment-mode structure protects against catastrophic failure but does not, on its own, prove the investment is paying back.

Use it also as a useful counterweight to JPMorgan COIN. Both are Augment-mode. COIN has a narrow, measurable productivity claim. Moderna has a broad, diffuse productivity claim. The narrower the claim, the easier the audit, the higher the confidence.
